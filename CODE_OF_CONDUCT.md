# Code of conduct

## Our commitment

We want GenOS to be a welcoming project for experienced systems programmers, first-time kernel contributors, designers, testers, writers, and curious users. Contributors are expected to make participation free from harassment regardless of experience, identity, background, disability, appearance, nationality, race, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, or other personal characteristics.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and specific when giving feedback.
- Critique code, design, and evidence—not the person presenting them.
- Welcome questions and explain project-specific assumptions.
- Acknowledge mistakes and correct them constructively.
- Keep technical disagreements focused on trade-offs and project goals.
- Respect privacy and coordinated security disclosure.

## Unacceptable behavior

- harassment, threats, discrimination, or sexualized attention;
- insults, personal attacks, or deliberate intimidation;
- publishing private information without permission;
- sustained disruption of discussions or project infrastructure;
- knowingly submitting malicious code or misleading security reports.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit, remove, or reject contributions and communication that violate these expectations. They may issue a warning, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently remove a participant depending on severity and history.

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through their GitHub profile. Reports will be reviewed as confidentially and promptly as practical. Maintainers must recuse themselves when they are directly involved in a report.

This policy applies in the GenOS repository, project communication spaces, and public situations where someone is representing the project.
